\textbf{Re: Associate Research Manager application}

Dear Admissions Committee, % OR To whom this may concern,


I am excited to apply for the doctoral fellowship at the Department of Economics at Ghent University, driven by a profound desire to deepen my research expertise and contribute to transformative solutions in Development Economics. As a Malawian Senior Research Associate with extensive international experience, having practiced research in two different continents; African and Asia, I offer a unique blend of practical research skills, leadership, and a commitment to addressing global challenges — qualities that will enrich the department while providing me with an exceptional platform to achieve my academic ambitions.  

The Department of Economics would benefit from my proven capacity to deliver rigorous, policy-relevant research. Currently, I lead the \href{https://poverty-action.org/philippines-socioeconomic-panel-survey-psps}{\color{UI_blue}Philippines Socioeconomic Panel Survey} at Innovations for Poverty Action (IPA). In this role I co-managed over 100 field staff for a survey across 500 communities and 15,000+ households, with responsibilities including questionnaire design, data collection, and quality assurance—demonstrating my ability to handle complex, cross-country survey demands. Additionally, I am overseeing a follow-up survey for an \href{https://poverty-action.org/combining-cash-grants-with-ultra-poor-livelihoods-training-program-philippines}{\color{UI_blue}RCT} with Principal Investigator Dean Karlan as well which assess whether combining a livelihoods training program with cash grants of varying sizes and business investment approaches enhances economic outcomes for ultra-poor households. I am also pioneering a feasibility study on WhatsApp surveys in the Philippines, demonstrating my innovative approach to research challenges. I have also previously worked on an RCT aimed at measuring \href{https://poverty-action.org/measuring-impact-aspirations-interventions-economic-and-psychosocial-well-being-philippines}{\color{UI_blue}the impact of aspirations interventions on economic and psychosocial well-being}.\par

My motivation to pursue a PhD stems from my identity as a troubleshooter, relentlessly driven to solve problems—whether untangling complex data analysis code or addressing research methodological challenges. I am eager to leverage this skill to tackle global issues, particularly poverty reduction, where rigorous research can yield transformative breakthroughs. This passion inspires me to further build my scholarly and research skills at Ghent University, enabling me to ask critical questions and uncover impactful answers in Development Economics—a field I am deeply committed to. The department’s world-class resources and collaborative environment will empower me to refine my expertise and contribute meaningfully to its academic community.  With a Master’s in Economics from the University of Malawi, where I excelled in quantitative methods and econometrics, and a track record of translating research into policy-relevant outputs, I am well-prepared to excel in this fellowship. 

Available to start on November 1, 2025, I am excited to bring my diverse experience to Ghent University.  \par


Thank you for considering my application. I am excited about the possibility of continuing to contribute to IPA's work and am available for an interview at your earliest convenience.
