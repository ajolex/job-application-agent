About Me

If you asked me to describe myself, I'd say I am analytical, adaptive, structured, pragmatic, and strategic. I apply this mindset to rigorous impact evaluation and data systems.

I specialize in the full research lifecycle; from designing RCT protocols and programming complex SurveyCTO instruments to conducting high-level econometric analysis in Stata and Python. My experience ranges from managing massive field operations (15,000+ household panel surveys at IPA) to leading technical market analysis that informs high-level policy (IFPRI).

But I am not just a technician. I focus on the "why" and "how" of evidence. How do we make rigorous methodology accessible? How do we ensure rigorous methods adapt to messy real-world implementation? I solve these problems to help organizations navigate uncertainty and drive meaningful social impact.

Beyond the data, I am deeply committed to capacity building and knowledge transfer. I believe that sustainable impact comes from empowering teams—mentoring junior researchers, streamlining data workflows, and fostering a culture of intellectual honesty where evidence is not just collected, but understood and owned by those who use it.
